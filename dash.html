Diagnosis — what's actually different

Hook Rate gap: Closers grab attention at 24–29% in the first 3 seconds. The trap ad ("Vid | Before/After | Male") shows 0% hook rate — the video almost never plays past the 3-second threshold but somehow drives form fills via thumbnail click. That's a curiosity lead, not a buying-intent lead.

Audience filtering at the creative level: Closers ("Video Monthly Price A", "VID | Implant | All sizes V3", "Video Cheap Dental Ads A") mention price, procedure, and financing in the opening frames. They self-select for people willing to engage with cost. The trap ad is a soft "before/after" reveal — entertainment, not commitment.

Hold Rate paradox: "All on 4 Dental Video – C" has a 46% hold rate (people watch most of the video) but produces 0 appointments. High engagement ≠ high intent — sometimes it's the inverse signal.

What this rules OUT: Not a CRM follow-up issue (the same sales team handles both groups). Not a geo issue (both run Phoenix +10mi). Not frequency saturation (frequency ~1.1 across the board).

Suggested actions

1

Switch optimization metric from CPL to Cost-per-Qualified-Appointment. Current campaigns optimize for the cheapest form fill, which is exactly what produces traps. Telling Meta to optimize for showed-up appointments forces it to learn the audience that completes the journey.

Estimated impact: 15–25% reduction in CPQL within 2 ad-set learning cycles (~14 days).
2

Cut "Video Cheap Dental Ads A" — it's a $9k drain. 13 appointments over L3M but only 1 proposal across 90 days. Reallocate its budget (≈$100/day) into "Video Monthly Price A" and "VID | Implant | All sizes V3" which are producing actual proposals.

Estimated impact: Reclaim ~$8,000/quarter in wasted spend; expect +2–4 proposals from reallocation.
3

Port closer hooks to the A/B test campaign. "Lead | Tof | Abo | Phoenix+10mi | A/B Test | 14/4/2026" is already showing a $278 CPQL with new creative. Test variants of "Video Monthly Price A" hook (the 29.4% hook rate is the highest in the account) against "VID | Implant | All sizes" hook.

Estimated impact: identify a hero hook within 14 days; scale to 50% of TOF spend if it wins.
4

Add a qualifying question to lead forms. Specifically: "Do you have $5,000+ available now for treatment?" This deliberately raises CPA from ~$50 to $150–250, but every form fill is then pre-qualified. The 100% qualification rate seen the week of May 10 ($55 CPQL!) suggests this lever already works in one ad set.

Estimated impact: CPQL improvement of 30–50% on tested ad sets.
5

Make Started Treatment the leading indicator, not Closed. 0 closes in 90 days makes Closed unusable as a feedback signal. Track Started Treatment (5 leads in May alone) and Treatment Proposal weekly. Once the sales process stabilizes, switch back to Closed.

Estimated impact: ad optimization regains feedback; expect creative learning rate to double.
6

Investigate the appointment-to-proposal collapse (CRITICAL). 82 appointments → 2 proposals over L3M is a 2.4% rate. This is NOT a Meta problem; it's a sales-process problem. Suggest auditing the first-day call script, no-show recovery flow, and proposal-handoff handoff. Without this fix, Meta efficiency gains have no downstream payoff.

Estimated impact: lifting Appt→Proposal from 2.4% to 15% (industry median for dental implants) would 6× revenue at the same ad spend.
