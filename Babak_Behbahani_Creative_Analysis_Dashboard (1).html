Classification definitions — how each ad gets labeled
Closer
Produced at least one treatment proposal. Rule: proposals ≥ 1 Why it matters: closers are the only ads with a proven path to revenue. Scale them.
Trap
Bargain trap — cheap appointments, no proposals. Rule: appts ≥ 3 AND cost-per-appt < $200 AND hook rate < 10% AND proposals = 0 Why it matters: cheap leads that never convert. The creative pulls clicks but doesn't communicate the offer, so leads arrive misaligned and the consult collapses. Kill.
Hold
Creative is working — proposal leak is downstream. Rule: hook rate ≥ 18% AND (appts > 0 OR hook ≥ 18%) AND proposals = 0 Why it matters: the ad is doing its job (people watch + click). Zero proposals means the leak is in the consult or the offer, not the creative. Watch — don't kill.
Dud
Below the creative floor — meaningful spend, no signal. Rule: hook rate < 10% AND spend > $500 AND proposals = 0 Why it matters: hook below 10% predicts zero proposals at any spend level. Kill immediately.
Dormant
Undersampled — not enough spend to judge. Rule: spend < $100 Why it matters: too small a sample to decide. Either give the ad more budget to test it, or leave alone.
The pattern: 3 of 40 ads (7.5%) carry the entire proposal pipeline. Price-led creative captures 52% of L3M spend ($18.6K) and produces both winners and the biggest loser. All-On-X creative is the most-tested theme (11 ads) but generates only 14 appointments total.

2The hook → proposal funnel

An illustrative diagram of where attention converts to revenue intent. Hook rate is the gate — below 18%, proposals do not happen.
The hook gate (24% threshold). Every ad that produced a proposal in L3M had a hook rate above 18%. The bargain trap shows what happens below that: the appointment is cheap because the lead clicked without watching the offer — they arrive misaligned and the consult collapses. Hook rate is upstream of money.
